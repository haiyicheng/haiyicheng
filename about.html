Hello! 👋

I'm a sociologist and graduate student at UNC-Chapel Hill studying how categorical distinctions structure and reproduce inequalities across populations.

I completed my B.A. in Politics, Economics, and Philosophy at Renmin University of China, where I developed expertise in quantitative sociology. My research applies advanced statistical methods to examine how state classifications, family environments, and institutional structures shape life chances and health outcomes.

Currently, I'm pursuing my M.A. at UNC while working at the Carolina Population Center. My research focuses on financial assets and cardiometabolic health, learning analytics validation, and comparative historical analysis of social stratification.
